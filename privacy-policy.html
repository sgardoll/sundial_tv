Sundial Privacy Policy

Effective March 29, 2026

Sundial is an astronomical clock for Apple TV that displays the current hour with real solar shadows. This policy explains what data the app accesses, how it is used, and what it does not do.

Location

If you grant permission, Sundial reads your device's approximate location (latitude and longitude) one time at launch. This data is used solely on your device to calculate the sun's position in the sky so the app can cast shadows in the correct direction. Your location is never transmitted off your device, never stored to disk, and never shared with anyone.

Location access is optional. If you decline or your device cannot provide a location, Sundial falls back to a simple time-based shadow that assumes a generic east-to-west sun path.

Font loading

Sundial uses the Google Fonts service to load the Playfair Display and JetBrains Mono typefaces. This causes a network request to Google's font servers on first launch. The request transmits only the font name and standard HTTP headers (your device's IP address, operating system, and app version). The app bundles these fonts locally as a fallback, so it works fully offline after the first load. Google's privacy policy for this service is available at policies.google.com/privacy.

What we do not collect

Sundial does not collect analytics or usage data. It does not use crash-reporting services. It does not display advertisements. It does not create user accounts. It does not use cookies, fingerprinting, or any form of tracking. It has no server component. It does not transmit any data to the developer.

Children's privacy

Sundial does not collect personal information from anyone, including children under 13. It complies with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and equivalent regulations in other jurisdictions.
